**Muesli Soaker**

* 100g Muesli (16.7% BP)
* 150g Guinness (25.0% BP)

**Yeast Bloom**

* 10g Water (1.7% BP)
* 3g Instant Dry Yeast (0.5% BP)

**Microwave Tangzhong**

* 140g Water (23.3% BP)
* 30g Bread Flour (5.0% BP)

**Main Dough**

* 290g Guinness (48.3% BP)
* 570g Bread Flour (95.0% BP)
* 16g Salt (2.7% BP)

---

#### Day 1: Prep & Bulk Fermentation

1. **Prepare the Soaker:** In a small container, combine 100g of muesli and 150g of the Guinness. Stir briefly, cover, and let it sit at room temperature to hydrate.
2. **Bloom the Yeast:** In a small ramekin, combine 10g of the water and 3g of instant dry yeast. Stir to moisten and set aside to proof.
3. **Microwave the Tangzhong:** In your final mixing bowl, whisk 140g of water and 30g of bread flour until smooth. Microwave in 15- to 20-second bursts, whisking thoroughly after each, until the mixture hits ~65°C (150°F) and forms a thick, glossy paste.
4. **Temper and Mix:** While the tangzhong is hot, slowly pour in the remaining 290g of Guinness while whisking constantly to create a smooth, lukewarm liquid base. Whisk in the bloomed yeast. Add the remaining 570g bread flour and 16g salt. Mix by hand until a shaggy dough forms. Rest for 30 minutes to hydrate the flour (Target temp: 24°C–25°C).
5. **Knead and Incorporate:** Knead the dough by hand for 4 minutes. Flatten the dough, spread the muesli soaker over the surface, and fold to enclose. Use the pincer method (repeatedly squeezing the dough between fingers) to thoroughly cut the grains into the dough matrix until evenly distributed.
6. **Initial Fermentation:** Cover and let rest at room temperature for 1.5 to 2 hours. Perform two sets of stretch-and-folds spaced 45 minutes apart to build structural tension.
7. **Cold Fermentation:** Cover the bowl tightly and transfer to the refrigerator for an overnight cold fermentation of 12 to 18 hours.

#### Day 2: Shaping & Baking

8. **Preheat:** 1 hour before baking, preheat the oven (ideally a steam oven) with a baking stone or steel to 240°C (465°F).
9. **Shape and Final Proof:** Turn the cold dough onto a minimally floured surface. Gently shape into a tight boule, building strong surface tension so it can stand freely. Place seam-side up in a floured banneton. Cover and let proof at room temperature for 1 to 2 hours (until it passes the poke test).
10. **Bake:** Turn the boule out, score the top, and load. Bake at 240°C (465°F) with steam for 20 minutes. Vent steam, reduce oven to 210°C (410°F), and continue baking for 25–40 minutes. The bread is finished when the crust is deeply browned and firm. Let cool completely on a wire rack.
