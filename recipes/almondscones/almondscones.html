Specialties-Style Almond Scones

Ingredients

Almond Paste (Yields ~100g)
40ggranulated sugar
50galmond flour
10gegg white (or water)
1mlalmond extract
1 pinchfine sea salt

Or substitute 100g store-bought firm almond paste.

Prep & Mix-Ins
120gsliced almonds
Dry Dough
320gall-purpose flour
65ggranulated sugar
15gbaking powder
3gfine sea salt
Fat & Liquids
140gcold unsalted butter
160mlheavy cream (cold)
1large egg (50g)
5mlalmond extract
Topping
15mlheavy cream
25gdemerara sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat & Prep: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toast the sliced almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat until fragrant. Transfer to a plate and freeze to cool completely.
  2. Almond Paste: If making from scratch, process 40g of granulated sugar in a high-powered blender until powdered. Add the almond flour, salt, 1ml extract, and egg white. Pulse until a firm paste forms. Wrap and chill in the freezer. If using store-bought paste, freeze it briefly to firm up for grating.
  3. Dry Mix: Place a large mixing bowl on a scale and tare. Measure the all-purpose flour, 65g granulated sugar, baking powder, and fine sea salt directly into the bowl. Whisk thoroughly.
  4. Cut in Fat: Cut the cold butter into small cubes. Add to the bowl and work it into the flour using your fingertips until it resembles coarse, pea-sized meal. Grate the chilled almond paste directly into the flour mixture, tossing gently to distribute. Fold in the cooled, toasted almonds.
  5. Add Liquids: Form a wide well in the center of the dry ingredients. Tare the scale again and measure the cold heavy cream, egg, and 5ml almond extract directly into the well. Use a fork to gently beat the liquid ingredients together within the well to break up the egg.
  6. Form Dough: Switch to a spatula and fold the wet and dry mixtures together just until a shaggy, slightly sticky dough forms. Turn out onto the counter, gently fold over itself 2 to 3 times to build layers, and press into a disk 2.5cm (1 inch) thick. Cut into 8 equal wedges.
  7. Chill: Transfer the wedges to a parchment-lined baking sheet. Freeze for 20 minutes to re-solidify the butter.
  8. Bake: Brush the tops of the chilled scones with the remaining 15ml heavy cream and sprinkle generously with demerara sugar. Bake for 18–22 minutes until highly puffed and deeply golden brown. Cool fully on a wire rack to allow the crumb structure to set.
