Braised Beef Shank Palak

Strongly Spiced • Bone-Free "Banana" Shanks • Labne Finish

Ingredients

The Aromatics & Spices
  • 250g Red onion, minced to a paste
  • 60g Fresh ginger, minced to a paste
  • 45ml Canola oil
  • 2 tsp Cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp Fennel seeds
  • 4 Dried red Thai chiles
  • 2 tbsp Coriander seeds, ground
  • 1 tsp Cayenne pepper
  • ½ tsp Ground turmeric
The Braise
  • 1.3kg Beef banana shanks, 3cm cubes
  • 800g (28oz) San Marzano tomatoes
  • 25g Beef bouillon paste
The Finish
  • 1kg Fresh spinach, washed & dried
  • 180g Labne, full-fat
Weights in metric; Temperatures in °F/°C.

Instructions

1. Setup & Aromatics Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Pulse onion and ginger in a processor until they form a coarse paste. Hand-crush the tomatoes in a bowl.
2. Tempering the Oil Heat oil in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add cumin, fennel, and chiles. Fry until they turn a dark, nutty brown (approx. 15s). Add the onion-ginger paste and sauté, stirring frequently, until the moisture evaporates and the edges turn deep golden brown (8–10m).
3. Concentrating the Masala Stir in the coriander, cayenne, turmeric, and beef bouillon paste. Add the crushed tomatoes. Simmer uncovered, stirring occasionally, for 12–15 minutes. The sauce is ready when it thickens significantly and oil begins to bead on the surface.
4. The Bhuna (Searing) Add the beef cubes to the pot. Increase heat slightly and stir-fry the meat in the tomato paste for 5 minutes. This sears the beef while bonding the spices to the muscle fibers.
5. The Braise Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid (or foil under the lid for a perfect seal). Transfer to the oven. Braise for 2.5 to 3 hours. The meat should yield completely to the back of a spoon.
6. Wilting the Spinach Return the pot to the stovetop over medium heat. Add spinach in three batches, folding gently to wilt. This preserves the "whole leaf" texture.
7. Labne Emulsion Whisk the labne in a small bowl. Temper by adding 3 tablespoons of the hot curry sauce to the labne, whisking until smooth. Fold this back into the curry. Remove from heat immediately and serve.
