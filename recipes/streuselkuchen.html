Streuselkuchen

Funeral Cake — Yields one 13" × 18" half-sheet pan

Ingredients

Streusel Topping

  • 200g butter, room temperature
  • 200g sugar
  • 250g all-purpose flour
  • 50g oat flour
  • 15ml vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1g salt

Yeast Dough

  • 350ml milk
  • 100g sugar
  • 10g SAF Gold instant yeast
  • 600g all-purpose flour
  • 90g butter, room temperature
  • 3g salt

Traditional Poppy Seed Paste (Optional)

  • 450g poppy seeds, ground
  • 680g honey
  • 160g semolina flour
  • 100g almond slivers
  • Water (as needed)

Instructions

  1. Prepare for Proofing: Heat the oven to 50°C / 120°F and then turn it off. If your oven does not hold a temperature this low, heat it slightly higher, turn it off, and crack the door open for a minute to let excess heat escape.
  2. Mix the Streusel: Place a non-metallic mixing bowl on a scale and tare to zero. Add the room-temperature butter, sugar, and roughly half of the all-purpose flour. Using a hand mixer, process until the butter is evenly incorporated. Tare the scale as needed to add the remaining all-purpose flour and oat flour, mixing until distinct crumbs form. Add the vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt. Transfer the crumbs to a separate container and set aside. Do not wash the mixing bowl.
  3. Activate the Yeast: Place the unwashed mixing bowl back on the scale, tare, and add the milk and sugar. Heat the milk mixture to body temperature (about 40 seconds in the microwave), then stir to dissolve the sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the milk, cover with plastic wrap, and let sit for about 15 minutes until frothy.
  4. Mix the Dough: Tare the scale and measure the flour, butter, and salt directly into the proofed yeast mixture. Using your hands, incorporate all the ingredients until the dough comes together in a cohesive ball. Remove the dough from the bowl and knead vigorously on a work surface for about 1 minute.
  5. First Proof: Form the dough into a ball, dust lightly with flour, and return it to the mixing bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rise in the warmed oven for about 30 minutes. It should approximately double in volume.
  6. Shape the Base: Roll out the dough and transfer it to the 13" × 18" cookie sheet. Make sure the dough covers the entire sheet evenly with no gaps around the edges.
  7. Final Proof: Evenly sprinkle the streusel crumbs over the dough. For a same-day bake, return the pan to the warm oven for 30–45 minutes until noticeably puffy. For an overnight retard, cover the pan tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 8 hours; let it sit at room temperature while the oven preheats before baking.
  8. Bake: Remove the pan from the oven if preheating is required. Set the oven to 165°C / 325°F. Bake in the middle rack for 35–40 minutes, or until the crust begins to turn a golden brown.
Variation Notes: If utilizing the poppy seed paste, layer it between the dough base and the streusel topping. Boil the ground poppy seeds for about 20 minutes with the honey, semolina flour, almond slivers, and just enough water to form a thick paste. Allow the paste to cool entirely to room temperature before spreading it onto the raw dough to avoid killing the yeast. Increase the total baking time by about 5 minutes.
