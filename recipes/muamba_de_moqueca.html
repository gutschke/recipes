Muamba de Moqueca

Beef, Poblano, & Japanese Sweet Potato Peanut Braise

A fusion of West African peanut stew and Brazilian Moqueca influences, utilizing the rich collagen of chuck roast and the creamy starch of Japanese sweet potatoes. Best served over fragrant ST25 Jasmine rice.

Ingredients

The Beef

  • 10g fine salt
  • 1000g chuck roast, 4cm cubes
  • 15g neutral cooking oil

Aromatics & Liquid

  • 250g yellow onion, diced
  • 150g poblano pepper, diced
  • 40g fresh ginger, minced
  • 30g garlic, minced
  • 1 Habanero pepper (pierced with a knife but left whole)
  • 85g tomato paste (approx. 1/2 can)
  • 500g coconut water
    (clear liquid, not canned coconut milk)
  • 250g water or light stock

The Roots & Finish

  • 400g Japanese sweet potato, peeled and cut into 3cm cubes
  • 200g natural, unsweetened peanut butter
  • 225g mixed power greens, roughly chopped
  • 15g fresh lime juice

Quick Reference

  • Steam Oven: 135°C (275°F) / 30% Humidity / Uncovered
  • Conv. Oven: 160°C (325°F) / Covered
  • Phase 1 (Beef): 1.5 hours
  • Phase 2 (+Roots): 45–60 mins
  • ST25 Rice Ratio: 1 part rice to 1.15 parts water (by volume)

Method

  1. Prepare the Emulsion Base: Place a medium mixing bowl on a scale, tare to zero, and weigh out the 200g of natural peanut butter. Set this aside for tempering later.
  2. Sear the Beef: Place a heavy-bottomed Dutch oven on the stove over high heat. Add the neutral oil. Sear the salted chuck roast cubes in batches to ensure deep browning on all sides. Remove the beef and set it aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.
  3. Build the Base: Lower the heat to medium. Add the diced onion and poblano pepper directly into the beef fat, scraping up any fond from the bottom. Sauté until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the ginger, garlic, and the whole pierced habanero. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the tomato paste and cook for another 2 minutes until it darkens slightly.
  4. The Initial Braise: Pour in the coconut water (ensuring it is thin, clear coconut water to prevent a greasy or broken sauce) and the additional water or stock. Return the seared beef and any resting juices to the pot. Bring to a gentle simmer on the stove. Transfer the Dutch oven into the preheated oven. Braise for 1.5 hours. (Leave uncovered if using a steam oven; cover tightly with a lid if using a conventional oven).
  5. Incorporate the Roots: Remove the pot from the oven and fold in the cubed Japanese sweet potato. Return to the oven for another 45 to 60 minutes, maintaining the same covered/uncovered status based on your oven type. The braise is ready when both the beef and the sweet potatoes are completely fork-tender. Remove from the oven and discard the whole habanero.
  6. Temper the Emulsion: Using a ladle, scoop approximately 300g of the hot braising liquid from the pot into your reserved bowl containing the pre-weighed peanut butter. Using a whisk or an immersion blender, vigorously blend the hot liquid into the peanut butter until it forms a completely smooth, stabilized emulsion. Pour this tempered mixture back into the main pot and stir gently to integrate.
  7. Wilt and Serve: Fold in the chopped power greens; the residual heat of the stew will wilt them within two minutes. Stir in the lime juice to brighten the final profile. Taste for salt, then serve immediately over ST25 Jasmine rice.
