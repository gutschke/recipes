Muamba de Moqueca

Beef, Poblano, & Japanese Sweet Potato Peanut Braise

A fusion of West African peanut stew and Brazilian Moqueca influences, utilizing the rich collagen of chuck roast and the creamy starch of Japanese sweet potatoes. Best served over fragrant ST25 Jasmine rice.

Ingredients

The Beef

  • 10g fine salt
  • 1000g chuck roast, 4cm cubes
  • 15g ghee
  • 3g annatto seeds (to approximate Dendê oil)

Aromatics & Liquid

  • 250g yellow onion, diced
  • 150g poblano pepper, diced
  • 40g fresh ginger, minced
  • 30g garlic, minced
  • 85g tomato paste (approx. 1/2 can)
  • 500ml coconut water
    (clear liquid, not canned coconut milk)
  • 250ml water or light stock

The Roots & Finish

  • 400g Japanese sweet potato, peeled and cut into 3cm cubes
  • 1 Habanero pepper (pierced with a knife but left whole)
  • 200g natural, unsweetened peanut butter
  • 225g mixed power greens, roughly chopped
  • 15g fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 15g fresh lime juice
  • 30g roasted peanuts, crushed (for garnish)

Quick Reference

  • Steam Oven: 135°C (275°F) / 30% Humidity / Uncovered
  • Conv. Oven: 160°C (325°F) / Covered
  • Phase 1 (Beef): 1.5 hours
  • Phase 2 (+Roots): 45–60 mins
  • ST25 Rice Ratio: 1 part rice to 1.15 parts water (by volume)

Method

  1. Prepare the Emulsion Base: Place a medium mixing bowl on a scale, tare to zero, and weigh out the 200g of natural peanut butter. Set this aside for tempering later.
  2. Infuse Fat & Sear the Beef: Place a heavy-bottomed Dutch oven on the stove over medium heat. Add the ghee and annatto seeds, blooming them for 1 to 2 minutes until the fat turns a deep, floral orange-red. Remove and discard the seeds with a slotted spoon. Increase the heat to high. Sear the salted chuck roast cubes in batches to ensure deep browning. Remove the beef and set it aside, leaving the infused fat in the pot.
  3. Build the Base: Lower the heat to medium. Add the diced onion and poblano pepper directly into the beef fat, scraping up any fond. Sauté until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the ginger and garlic; cook for 1 minute. Add the tomato paste and cook until it turns a deep, rust-red color (the pincé technique), about 3–4 minutes, to develop its umami depth.
  4. The Initial Braise: Pour in the coconut water and the additional water or stock. Return the seared beef and any resting juices to the pot. Bring to a gentle simmer on the stove. Transfer the Dutch oven into the preheated oven. Braise for 1.5 hours. (Leave uncovered if using a steam oven; cover tightly with a lid if using a conventional oven).
  5. Incorporate the Roots: Remove the pot from the oven. Fold in the cubed Japanese sweet potato and nestle the whole pierced habanero into the liquid. Return to the oven for another 45 to 60 minutes. Delaying the habanero prevents it from disintegrating over the full cook time and spiking the heat. The braise is ready when both the beef and sweet potatoes are fork-tender. Remove the pot and discard the whole habanero.
  6. Temper the Emulsion: Using a ladle, scoop approximately 300ml of the hot braising liquid from the pot into your reserved bowl containing the pre-weighed peanut butter. Using a whisk or an immersion blender, vigorously blend the hot liquid into the peanut butter until it forms a completely smooth, stabilized emulsion. Pour this tempered mixture back into the main pot and stir gently.
  7. Wilt and Serve: Fold in the chopped power greens and cilantro; the residual heat will wilt them within two minutes. Stir in the lime juice. Taste and adjust salt only at this stage, as the long reduction concentrates flavors. Serve immediately over ST25 Jasmine rice, garnished with the crushed roasted peanuts for texture.
